ABOUT ST. MARY'S SCHOOL

Established in 2000 ZOROASTRIAN CHARITABLE EDUCATION TRUST(ZCET) is registered under the Bombay Public Trust Act of 1950.

 

THE ST. MARYS SCHOOL is managed and run by ZCET in a small tribal town of Dahod in Gujarat.

 

It was Established in  the year 2000 by a old Parsi family settled in Dahod for more than 300 years. The kindergarten section started in 2000 and the St Mary's School Primary section started in June 2002.

 

Established on the concept of Philanthropy and Universal Love for Mankind. ZCET caters to providing Quality Education with

the Best Facilities at a very minimised cost. If the name Parsi is synonymous with charity the Zoroastrian Charitable

Education trust would stand in the forefront for the same in this backward and tribal region of Dahod.

 

The Trust and the School works on the Principles of Truthfulness, Charity, Skill, Endeavour and giving encouragement to

others in good deeds.

 

Prophet  Zarathustra's command of "Happiness comes to him who seeks happiness for others" is followed in its true meaning

by the trust and the school managed by the trust.

 

The Trust works on the motto of Good Thoughts, Good Words and Good Deeds, which is also the School Motto.

 

We believe the biggest strength and asset are its children, meaningful education to the child is the nucleus of all change.

ST. MARY'S SCHOOL QUARTERLY REPORT, ACADEMIC YEAR 2013-13, APRIL TO AUGUST

The new academic year commenced in the month of April, filled with enthusiasm and eagerness. The students and teachers; all

geared up to embark upon an illuminating voyage of productive activities, events and holistic learning.

With the plenteous support and appreciation of the parents, their co-operation and the determination of our staff members, we have

achieved our goals.

We present a synopsis of our advances and accomplishments from the month of April to August 2013-13.

Orientation Programmes

01

We began the new term with Orientation and induction programmes for the parents of all Classes Junior Kindergarten to XII.

 

The parents were briefed about the history of the school, its growth and development, over the past decade.

 

The parents were introduced to the teachers, who would be mentoring their children through the new academic year. The CCE

system with its multifarious activities was explained to the parents in much detail with demonstrations. They were also informed

how the facilitators would guide and support their children ensuing complete learning and growth. They were made aware of the

basic school rules and regulations, familiarizing them with the school protocols of leave, absenteeism and various important

aspects of functioning.

02

Infrastructural Growth : A Turn - Over for the Primary Section

The primary section, Classes I to V, have been equipped and converted into, a state of the art multimedia classrooms. Multimedia

stimulates more than one sense at a time, resulting in students being more engrossed and more attentive. The teachers progress

into facilitators by incorporating multimedia in their daily teaching and instructions, students are engaged in effective learning, as

difficult concepts are explained through various audio and visual presentations which builds curiosity and captivates their minds,

it inspires creativity, and above all they have fun. 

03

Academics

Our School is based on Integrated Learning Approach which promotes creativity, curiosity and self-reliance. It is an activity based

learning where experimentation, self-discovery and hands on experience are given more importance. This inter-disciplinary

approach creates eagerness and these active learners are exposed to a stimulating learning environment and engage in a wide range

of development combined with various activities. We prepare children for academic success and growth in middle school and

beyond. Our facilitators follow a personalized approach for each student, priming them to cope with the demands of a changing

world.  The motto is to ensure the growth of learners all through these years guiding them to understand the basic concepts. An

exclusive and well-designed curriculum (NCERT-CBSE) framed for the school helps to maintain high academic standards to face

the outside world with confidence and innovative thinking processes.

ASSESSMENT

Our curriculum is graded and divided, broken down into various segments; each segment is a base for the next segment. Each

segment is assessed through a student centred formative activity, creating a strong foundational base. This has reduced the

stress levels, work load and above all rote learning.

 

Each discipline is divided into various segregated skills, which can be acquired effortlessly by the learner.

Primary Section : 

We have no examinations and pass/fail criteria for the primary section. Our students are evaluated through three observations

which are not time bound. The facilitators observe the child’s performance and learning abilities through strict protocols and

checklists, and evaluated continuously through class assignments, projects and activities, during the course of the year. At

regular intervals of 4 months, a detailed profile of the child’s performance is given to the parent, which reflects the child’s

strong and weak skills. This continuous comprehensive assessment is a true manifestation every child’s progress.

Middle Section and Secondary Section : 

We follow the Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation system as specified by the CBSE. It refers to a system of school-

based evaluation of the students that covers all aspects of students’ development. The scheme also covers both the scholastic

and the co-scholastic aspects of students’ growth and development with the help of variety of tools and techniques. Formatives

and Summative are carried out throughout the academic session at regular intervals with the weightage specified by CBSE.

 

The students are graded on a nine point rating scale – the scholastic areas and co-scholastic on a five point   rating scale

specified by CCE scheme. They will also be assessed for work experience, Art Education and physical and Education. In Co-

scholastic areas, the students are assessed in Life Skills, Attitudes and Values, Literary -Creative skills and Eco-Clubs, Sports

and Yoga.

Senior Secondary Section : 

In the last academic session that is 2012-13, we started the first batch of Senior Secondary section with commerce and science

streams. 

Scheme of Assessments VI to X : 

CCE reduces stress as it is not 100% exam oriented- students are assessed on scholastic and non-scholastic areas. CCE focuses

on overall development of the student’s personality and not on rote learning.

 

  1. The Academic Year is divided into two semesters/terms:

    1. Term/Semester I- April to September

    2. Term/Semester II- October to March

 

  1. Each semester will have two assessments called Formative Assessments(FA) which will be a series of appraisals and evaluations in the form of:           

    1. Projects or Investigatory work.

    2. Laboratory Manuals and Journals

    3. Group Discussions, Debates, Elocutions and Viva-Voce

    4. Home Assignments- Map work, Problem solving, Research-work, Reference work.

    5. Written Assessment: Class Tests, MCQ’s, Objectives

School Health Screening 

School health screenings are a sure way to confirm the wellness of every student and the best way to detect problems that may

interfere with a student’s education and development. The school organizes a comprehensive health and wellness diagnosing

twice a year by a certified and experienced practitioner twice in a year. Every student from Kindergarten to Senior Secondary

undergoes an extensive checkup, following strict protocols. Appropriate referrals are made to the students’ parents as and when

needed. A record is maintained for students with infirmities.

potential to nurture positive school culture and develops interpersonal intelligence. Our school assemblies, includes our Acts of

Worship to strengthen and sensitize the foundation of secularism. Our students and staff, make contributions through their

presence and participation.

Club Activities

  • ECO-CLUB :

 

The school has collaborated with CSE which conducts the “green School Programme every year. The GSP is an audit or an

inspection of the school done by the students on its environmental practices. The five spheres which are audited are; Air, Water,

Land, Energy, & Waste. The school is an exemplar, and has scored the second position continuously for the last three years.

 

  • LITERARY SKILLS CLUB :

 

This club helps students to sharpen their skills as writers/poets/creative journalists and translators. Students can pursue their

interests in becoming a person of good writing proficiency or wordsmiths, be it as a creative fictionist or as an excellent story

teller. Various literary skills activities are conducted; Story Writing, Poetry Writing, Story Translation, and Speech Delivery. The

school has enrolled with the organization “Katha” and soon will boast of its own “Katha Club”, which is a new initiative by

CBSE recently.

Martial Arts

Martial Arts training provide a child with a number of useful tools, apart from learning how to fight and defend themselves.

 Practicing Martial Arts will undoubtedly condition and improve the student’s physical strength and overall fitness. When a

student knows that they can defend themselves when need arises, a special kind of confidence level are achieved which radiates

to every aspect of life. They learn the skills and practices to form strong mind and body connection.  Regular training teaches the

student to develop an unbreakable focus and concentration which again carries over to other areas of leading a well-balanced life.

 

The school is associated with Wado Ryu Karate Do Federation and the school boasts of 118 yellow belts, 25 Orange belts, 15

Green Belts, 05 Blue Belts, 01 Purple Belt and 05 Brown Belts 4th Degree karate students.

 

SELF DEFENSE WORKSHOP :

 

The skills taught in Karate are focused towards self-defense and self-awareness, they enable a child to handle and face tough

situations, while, keeping a calm mental state.

 

The school conducted a special self-defense workshop for girls where 100+ girls from class V onwards were given training in self-

defense, by the Dahod Distt. Police and the Karate Instructor.

SCHOOL MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE

Every student’s life is a blank page; each of us educators, write our “story” in their lives… a story full of knowledge, inspiration, skills and character; an ability to reason than to assume.

“The only purpose of education is to teach a student how to live his life-by developing his mind and equipping him to deal with reality. The training he needs is theoretical, i.e., conceptual. He has to be taught to think, to understand, to integrate, to prove. He has to be taught the essentials of the knowledge discovered in the past-and he has to be equipped to acquire further knowledge by his own effort.”

OUR SCHOOL RULES

"Pupil's Code of Conduct"
 

School has profound faith in self discipline as it believes that discipline is essential in all walks of life. It is an integral tool for the development of personality. The measures outlined are of self help variety and serve to inculcate an awareness in :

         
         DRESS APPEARANCE
         TURNOUT 
 

  • All pupils are expected to be neat and clean and wear prescribed school uniform.

  • Shoes need to be well polished.

  • Girls are advised to dress their hair into one plait using black bands. Girls with short hair are advised to wear black hair bands.

  • Fancy / Ornamental hairdos / clips are not allowed. School forbids girls to wear big earrings and use of  nail polish and mehndi. 

01

  • ​Students must converse in English during school hours, they will be fined if caught speaking in any other language.

  • Courtesy and politeness will be the hallmark of the pupils who must greet all staff members, visitors and elders, showing due respect at all times.

  • Classroom discipline should be maintained.

  • Pupils must avoid loud talk in and out of classes, and also chorus answers. Silence in corridors, in the library and laboratories is a must.

  • Damage to school property will be penalized.

  • Litter must be put into litter bins / dustbins and waste
    paper baskets.

02

BEHAVIOUR / DEPORTMENT / SPEECH
ATTANDANCE / LEAVE / ABSENCE 

03

  • ​As regular attandence is an important element of education no leave or absent will be granted except for serious reasons.

  • Attandence on opening and closing days of the terms is compulsory.

  • Every pupil is required to put in attendance of not less than 90% of the total number of working days.

  • Parents must state early the reason for the absence such as religious celebration, marriages etc. failure to do so invalidates the application.

  • Appointment with doctors must not be taken during school hours

  • In case of serious illness the principal must be informed within five days with a Doctor's certificate along with the leave application by the parent.

  • No students shall be allowed to leave the school premises during the school hours.

  • Irregular attandence, habitual idleness, disobedience or defiance of authority, lack of sufficient improvement in studies and conduct even after warnings or conduct injurious to the moral tone of the school are sufficient reasons for expulsion of a student.

Rights Reserved

01

All terms and conditions, the school fees, or other charges mentioned on the school notice board and informed through the diary or fee slip are valid for the relevant academic year only. The school reserves the right to amend any of these, fee structures or terms and condition for any subsequent academic year.

02

Deposit of Fees and other charges

  • All School Fees are to be paid before the mentioned payment dates for that term. Each Academic year consist of two terms, and each term consists of six months.

  • Fee are to be deposited at the designated bank branches only. Fee slips are issued at the beginning of every term.

  • It is the responsibility of the parent to ensure that all school fees are paid within the stipulated dates and the school may not remind them of the unpaid fees.

  • Late payment charges are applicable on all school fees payment if they are not paid before the given dates.The school will not entertain any reasons for late payment.

  • Payments can be made via bank draft or cheques. Payment via bank draft or cheque shall be made out in favor of "ST MARY'S SCHOOL".

  • Administration charges of Rs 500.00 will be imposed for unsuccessful fee transaction or cheques that are not honoured by the bank for any reasons.

  • The school reserves the right to deduct all unpaid fees and charges. The transfer certificate will be issued only when all dues are cleared.

  • In case of mid term withdrawal of a student, the school authority should be informed in writing one month in advance. Fees for the month in which the student leaves will have to be paid in full.

  • Transfer certificate would not be issued unless all the dues of the school are settled.

  • The school reserves the right to withhold examination results (Internal or external Board results) and shall prevent the student / candidate from attending or appering for any examination conducted by the school in the event of non-payment of any dues to the school.

  • The school shall NOT be responsible if fees are not deposited on time. Parents are expected to follow all guidelines and pay the fees on time.

03

Medical

  • The parent / guardian shall declare to school in writing, at the time of admission any/all serious illness, disability physical or mental, and/or health condition and/or allergy of the child.

  • The parent shall also inform the school in writing from time to time, as and when the child is diagnosed of any serious illness, disability physical or mental, and/or health condition and/or allergy.

  • The school shall not be held responsible for any medical exigencies that invole special medical intervention in relation to the special or serious illness, disability physical or mental, and/or health condition and/or allergy of the child.

04

Absence

Students who have been sick should bring a medical certificate from their doctor on joining school. Repeated absence without leave or unexplained absence for more than one month renders the students name to be stck off the rolls. Strict action will be taken for those students who are absent for total of four or more days in a month. Half day / Early leave is strictly prohibited. In case of emergency please contact school office.

05

Withdrawal

Students can be asked to leave the school on the following grounds:

  • Disciplinary grounds

  • Unsatisfactory process in academics

  • Attendance below 75%
